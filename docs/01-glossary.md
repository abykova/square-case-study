# 01 — Security Glossary

This glossary defines key security terms used in this case study.

- **Asset** — anything of value to the organization that needs protection
  (e.g., customer PII, account balances, payment transactions).

- **Threat** — a potential cause of an unwanted incident,
  which may result in harm to a system or organization
  (e.g., external attacker, malicious insider, malware).

- **Vulnerability** — a weakness that can be exploited by a threat
  (e.g., missing access control check, weak configuration).

- **Risk** — combination of the likelihood of a threat exploiting
  a vulnerability and the impact on the business.

- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)** — any data that can identify
  a specific individual (e.g., full name, phone number, email, national ID).

- **Confidentiality** — property that information is not made available
  or disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes.

- **Integrity** — property of accuracy and completeness of information and processing.

- **Availability** — property of being accessible and usable upon demand
  by an authorized entity.

This glossary is used as a shared reference for all SQUARE steps.
